I am an assistant professor at the Department of Computer Science, NYU Shanghai (Starting at Fall 2023). I am also affiliated at Center for Data Science, New York University.
I was a postdoctoral researcher at the Department of Computer Science, New York University, mentored by Prof. Kyunghyun Cho and Prof. He He. I am a member of the ML2 lab and the CILVR group.
I received my Ph.D. at the Department of Computer Science, University of Maryland, College Park, advised by Prof. Jordan Boyd-Graber and Prof. Hal Daumé III. Throughout my graduate studies, I spent several summers doing internships in industry, including Microsoft Semantic Machines, DMX group and IDEAS group at Microsoft Research.
My main research interest is in natural language processing. My goal is to equip intelligent agents (e.g., Language Models) with external tools to make them more reliable and interpretable in real scenarios. Some directions I'm mostly excited nowadays include:

(1) Reasoning-intensive Retrieval and augmented Generation: How to find and incorporate external knowledge into language models to satisfy complex user needs (e.g., find a code snippet or a theorem) that often require intensive reasoning capacities?

(2) Controllable Text Generation: How to generate text (e.g., provide long-form answer to a question) that satisfies interlocking constraints: being controllable and truthful?

(3) Understanding how Language Models aquire knowledge: How current language models internally aquire knowledge, and adopt quickly with new information (i.e. Knowledge Editing)?

(4) Human-centered NLP in Healthcare: How to have both language model and human in-the-loop to reliably solve real-world healthcare problems (e.g., Radiology Report Generation)?
